These are the final category values intended for `Detailed info.csv`.

Report types:
- statement: recorded testimony, declaration, or first-person account.
- correspondence: any official letter, telegram, memo, recommendation, forwarding note, or investigative office communication.

Crime type:
- kidnapping: use when the page says the person was abducted, seized, carried off, or stolen away against their will, often from home, family, tribe, or native place.
- illegal detention: use when the page says the person was kept, confined, imprisoned, or prevented from leaving, especially when the focus is on unlawful holding rather than sale or transport.

Conflict type:
- manumission dispute: whether the person should be freed, granted a manumission certificate, or recognized as entitled to release.
- ownership dispute: who owns or claims the person, including competing masters or contested possession.
- debt dispute: a debt, repayment claim, or financial obligation affecting the person's status, holding, or transfer.
- free-status dispute: whether the person is already free, was wrongly treated as enslaved, or should be recognized as not legally enslaved.
- forced-transfer dispute: a dispute over the person being taken, sent, or moved against their will from one place or controller to another.
- repatriation dispute: whether, where, or how the person should be sent back or allowed to return to a home place, homeland, or chosen destination.
- kidnapping case: the matter is framed primarily as a kidnapping or abduction case, rather than only a later ownership or manumission issue.

Trial type:
- manumission requested: the person asked for freedom or sought release, but the page does not specifically mention a certificate outcome.
- freedom/manumission outcome: any official step, decision, or completion related to a person's freedom, manumission, or recognition as free. This includes recommendation, approval, grant, confirmation of free status, release, discharge, liberty, and a related certificate being sent, forwarded, or delivered.
- repatriation arranged: arrangements were made to send the person back or forward them to a home or chosen destination.

Whether abuse:
- yes: direct evidence of abuse, beating, violence, cruelty, or similar mistreatment.
- no: the page clearly indicates no abuse or gives direct reason to mark no.
- empty string: the page does not support either yes or no.
