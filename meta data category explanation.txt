Report types:

* `statement`: recorded testimony, declaration, or first-person account

* `correspondence`: any official letter, telegram, memo, recommendation, forwarding note, or investigative office communication.


Crime Type:
* `kidnapping`: use when the page says the person was **abducted, seized, carried off, or stolen away** against their will, often from home, family, tribe, or native place.

* `illegal detention`: use when the page says the person was **kept, confined, imprisoned, or prevented from leaving**, especially when the focus is on unlawful holding rather than sale or transport.



Conflict type:
* `manumission dispute`: use when the page is about whether the person should be **freed, granted a manumission certificate, or recognized as entitled to release**.

* `ownership dispute`: use when the main issue is **who owns or claims the person**, including competing masters or contested possession.

* `debt dispute`: use when the case centers on a **debt, repayment claim, or financial obligation** affecting the person’s status, holding, or transfer.

* `free-status dispute`: use when the issue is whether the person is **already free, was wrongly treated as enslaved, or should be recognized as not legally enslaved**.

* `forced-transfer dispute`: use when the page centers on a dispute over the person being **taken, sent, or moved against their will** from one place or controller to another.

* `repatriation dispute`: use when the main issue is whether, where, or how the person should be **sent back or allowed to return** to a home place, homeland, or chosen destination.

* `kidnapping case`: use when the page frames the matter primarily as a **kidnapping/abduction case**, rather than just a later ownership or manumission issue.



Trial type:
* manumission requested`: use when the page says the person **asked for freedom** or sought release, but does not specifically mention a certificate.
*freedom/manumission outcome: use when the page indicates any official step, decision, or completion related to a person’s freedom, manumission, or recognition as free. This includes cases where manumission is recommended, approved/granted, or the person’s free status is confirmed; where the person is released, discharged, or set at liberty; and where a related certificate is sent, forwarded, or delivered.
*repatriation arranged`: use when the page says arrangements were made to **send the person back** or forward them to a home or chosen destination.
